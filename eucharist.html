A Case for the Eucharist

An Exploration of the Real Presence

In Progress

About This Book

This work examines the theological, historical, and scriptural foundations for the doctrine of the Real Presence in the Eucharist. Drawing from early Church writings, biblical exegesis, and the continuity of Christian tradition, it makes a comprehensive case for understanding the Eucharist as truly the Body and Blood of Christ.

Overview

The book explores several key areas:

Biblical Foundation — A careful examination of John 6, the Last Supper accounts, and Paul's first letter to the Corinthians, showing how the earliest Christian communities understood Christ's words about his flesh and blood.

Historical Evidence — Tracing belief in the Real Presence through the writings of the Church Fathers, from Ignatius of Antioch and Justin Martyr through Augustine and beyond, demonstrating the unbroken tradition of Eucharistic theology.

Theological Development — How the Church's understanding of transubstantiation developed not as innovation but as articulation of what had always been believed, addressing common objections and misunderstandings.

Contemporary Relevance — Why the Eucharist matters today and how recovering this understanding transforms Christian worship and life.
